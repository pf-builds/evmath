How this is calculated

Your gas vehicle premium is multiplied by a percentage range anchored to Insurify's EV Premium Penalty report: about 15-25% higher for EVs model year 2024 or newer, 30-49% higher for older models, and the full 15-49% span if you're not sure. The gap is shown as a range because your specific insurer, state, and vehicle matter more than any industry average.

Why EVs cost more to insure

EVs typically cost more to repair than comparable gas vehicles, driven by specialized parts and labor, battery pack repairs, and the sensor and camera recalibration that advanced driver-assistance systems often require after even minor collisions. Bankrate, citing CCC Intelligent Solutions repair data, reports the average EV repair bill runs over $1,030 higher than a comparable gas-vehicle repair. Insurers price premiums around expected claim cost, so higher typical repair bills show up as higher premiums, the same dynamic behind the dealer-versus-independent cost gap on the repair cost estimator.

Why model year matters so much

Insurify's data shows the EV insurance cost gap narrowing substantially for newer model years, down to about 18% versus a 42% average across all model years. That's attributed to broader adoption of driver-assistance safety technology and insurers gaining more claims data on newer EV models specifically, rather than pricing them as an unknown risk.

What this doesn't account for

This doesn't account for your specific insurer, state, driving record, credit-based insurance score where allowed, or the specific EV model, all of which affect your real premium far more than the EV-versus-gas difference alone. Some individual EV models are already at or below their gas equivalents. Get actual quotes from your insurer for both vehicles before deciding.
