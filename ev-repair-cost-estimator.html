How this is calculated

Each repair type has a cost range for a dealer or certified shop and a separate range for an independent shop. The gap between them reflects published research on labor rate differences, generally in the 20-40% range according to comparisons like Consumer Reports' repair shop survey, though it narrows or disappears for repairs where independent shops have limited parts access regardless of labor rate. A parts-access note is shown alongside every range for that reason. These figures are wide by design, covering the real spread from compact EVs to premium ones, not a single vehicle.

Why independent isn't always cheaper

Labor rates are often lower at independent shops, but many EV-specific parts, especially battery modules and drive units, are proprietary and either unavailable outside the dealer network or must still be sourced through the dealer regardless of where the labor happens. That narrows or eliminates the savings for those particular repair types. Wear items like brakes, suspension components, and the 12V auxiliary battery are standard automotive parts with much wider independent availability and closer to the full published labor-rate discount.

Why parts availability varies by manufacturer and region

Some manufacturers make EV-specific parts and diagnostic tools available to independent and aftermarket shops, others largely restrict them to their own dealer network. This varies further by whether a given independent shop has invested in EV-specific training and equipment, and by region as the used and aftermarket EV parts market matures unevenly.

Independent service and your warranty

Under the federal Magnuson-Moss Warranty Act, a manufacturer cannot void your vehicle's warranty solely because you used an independent shop for a repair unrelated to a warranty claim. Having brakes or suspension serviced independently, for example, cannot legally void a separate battery or drivetrain warranty. It's still worth confirming with your specific manufacturer's warranty terms.

What this doesn't account for

This doesn't account for whether the repair might be covered under your vehicle's warranty, check your specific coverage before assuming you'll pay out of pocket. It also doesn't include towing or a rental car, and it uses general cost ranges rather than a quote for your specific vehicle. Always get an actual quote before committing to a repair.
