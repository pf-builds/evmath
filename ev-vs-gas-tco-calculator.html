How this is calculated

Each vehicle's subtotal is purchase price plus energy or fuel cost over the full ownership period plus maintenance over the same period. Energy cost is annual mileage divided by efficiency, times your electricity rate, times years owned. Fuel cost is annual mileage divided by MPG, times gas price, times years owned. The resale value delta, if entered, is applied once to the EV's total to reflect an expected difference in what each vehicle is worth at the end of the period.

Where these numbers come from

Vehicle efficiency and MPG figures are published by the EPA and DOE at fueleconomy.gov. Electricity and gas prices are tracked nationally by the U.S. Energy Information Administration (EIA), but vary enough by region that this calculator asks for your own local numbers instead of a national average. Maintenance defaults are rough starting points, published maintenance cost studies (for example, from Consumer Reports or AAA's annual driving cost research) can give you a more specific number for your vehicles.

Why the EV can cost more upfront but less over time

EVs commonly carry a higher purchase price than a comparable gas vehicle, but lower per-mile energy cost and less routine maintenance can close or reverse that gap over years of ownership. How fast that happens depends entirely on your mileage, local electricity and gas prices, and how long you keep the vehicle, which is exactly what this calculator is solving for using your own numbers.

What this doesn't account for

This doesn't model loan interest, insurance cost differences, or state and federal EV purchase incentives, all of which can shift the comparison. It also doesn't include home charging equipment cost, use the home charging install payback calculator for that. Resale value is a single user-entered delta rather than a modeled depreciation curve for each vehicle.

There's no universal number for this, resale value depends on the specific models, mileage, battery health, and the used market at the time of sale. Checking a resale value guide for your specific EV and gas vehicle model is more reliable than a general assumption.
